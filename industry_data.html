How do each of these rank in terms of demand from employers as a job title.



When considering IT as a industry, it has wide range of sub divisions where each subdivision is required specific skill sets to perform.
All these sub parts of IT industry is required unique skill sets to perform the relevant job or tasks.
Therefore, different job category and skills has different demand in the market.
In order to get a better understand about the market demand for aforementioned mentioned job categories,
need to be analysed the actual demand for particular jobs in the market.
To get an overall understand about the demand Burning Glass Technologies report on Labour Insight Jobs has been taken into consideration during this analysis.
According to the report, there is high demand for solution architect where there is 8% of demand out of overall job vacancies published in IT industry.
When considering about aforementioned job titles (Group D), penetration tester has the highest demand where Senior test analysis got lowest 4% and 2% respectively.
Both python technical developer and Full stack develop have 3% demand out of total vacancies posted during the period.

Penetration tester



Penetration tester is who will perform authorised tests on computer systems in order to expose weaknesses in their security
that could be exploited by criminals. You can choose to specialise in manipulating a particular type of system,
such as: networks and infrastructures. Windows, Linux and Mac operating systems.

IT skills

- Strong Working Knowledge of Networking and Network Protocols: By definition, understanding how hackers and cybercriminals operate
requires penetration testers to understand networking and network protocols like TCP/IP, UDP, ARP, DNS, and DHCP.
- Deep Knowledge of Exploits and Vulnerabilities: Most employers prefer candidates whose knowledge of vulnerabilities and exploits
goes beyond automated approaches.
- Complete Command of Operating Systems: Penetration testing professionals need advanced knowledge of the operating systems
they attempt to hack or breach in conducting their assessments.

Generic skills

- Report Writing: Strong writing skills serve penetration testing professionals well because their duties
include producing reports for management and executive teams to review
- Communicating Up, Down, and Across All Levels of the Organization.
- Supporting and Managing Security Services
- Up-To-Date on Relevant
