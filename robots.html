Robots

What does it do? (600)

At this stage of our time robotics are being developed to be an integral part of our society. For example, a small company called Hyundai Boston Dynamics
which was started by MIT in 1992 and now owned by the Hyundai Motor Group have developed quite amazing robots.
One of the robots already in the market is a robot calls Spot. Spot is a robotic dog that can navigate terrain with unprecedent mobility.
Spot allows companies to use this robot as a routine inspection companion. And I say companion because Spot is neither a human nor a tool like a computer.
Spot can be used in high security environments, dangerous work environments such as construction or rescue missions to assist in searching.
The design of Spot has also been used in a television series called “War of the Worlds” where an alien civilization has released these robot dogs to attack humans.
This brings up the question of the ethical standards of robotics and how they should be implemented. What rules should a robot follow? And what if a robot was developed
to have a mind or ability to make decisions for itself? We have all seen the film Terminator – an artificially intelligent robot from the future that travels
back in time to attack a woman's unborn son not once but twice with Terminator 2. In his science fiction book by Isac Asimov wrote about three important laws for
robotics which have become widely regarded as an important guide for those in the future who may develop a robot and artificial
intelligence capable of making its own free will and decisions.
These three laws are:

First Law
A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm.

Second Law
A robot must obey the orders given it by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law.

Third law
A robot must protect its own existence if such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law.

Most interestingly these laws were used in the 1991 film iRobot where a robot was developed without the three laws of robotics and had a far more advanced brain
system allowing it to dream and even create artworks and even project or “feel” emotion. As the film progressed the other robots were controlled by a supercomputer
that went rogue and turned all other modern robotic law-abiding robots against humanity. In the end the robot with the three laws removed was set free and left to rule
over all remaining robots on the outskirts of the city. This raised the great question can robots and humans live in harmony?

Robots are not going anywhere in fact Hyundai Boston Dynamics are also developing military grade robots that can also with stand heavy firepower
from an enemy without hurting a single human being. Their product still in development called Atlas is the world's most dynamic humanoid
robot and can even compete with fit athletic humans in agility and running and movement on almost any terrain. Atlas is still a research product
in development using hydraulic actuation with 28 different joints as well as an advanced LiDAR system for balance.
However, when Atlas becomes a mainstream product, our world will change. Some for the better and some for the worst.

What is the likely impact (300)

Robots will likely impact humanity in both a positive way and a negative way.
Firstly, in the negative way. Robots will take manual labor jobs from humans.
Trade positions like brick layers, builders, electricians, and retail positions such as retail assistants, booth operators on train stations, security guards,
and many other will cause the population to lose their jobs. However, it will be important for government to write laws that protect human jobs.
Hawaii state law makers in the US has voted to explore ways how to protect the population from robots taking jobs by introducing a universal income.

While robots may have a negative impact on humanity there are also several positive ways that robots can help humans.
The positive aspect of introducing robots means that humans can focus more on their health and wellbeing and allow robots to do all the
arduous work for us. Robots are already being developed in the medical field where extremely accurate lifesaving surgery can mean the difference between life and death.
The Da Vinci Systems robot allows doctors to perform these accurate robotic assisted minimally invasive surgeries and procedures reducing the risk of death or error by the surgeon.
In addition to this, robots have already begin impacting manufacturing by using co-bots building our vehicles.
Transportation and shopping are already using drones to deliver order efficiently to customers reducing wait times to receive packages.
Amazon uses robots to search and prepare our orders in massive warehouses and brings the product to a human for packaging and delivery.
Soon we will see the introduction of robotic vehicles where no driver is present.
And this will allow governments to create better algorithms to reduce traffic jams and accidents in large cities.

How will this affect you? (300)

For me I see robots as an exciting future for humanity.
I believe that robots will make our lives better by helping us do the mundane tasks we do not always want to do or simply do not have time for.
For example, at home we may have a robot that cleans the floors mows the lawns and a robot that does the washing and general house cleaning.
As artificial intelligence becomes more advanced robots will be more like a personal home assistant rather than a machine that we turn on and off as we need.
I see robots affecting me in a positive way as by the time I am near death a robot may well be taking care of me in my home during my last days.
The robot would be able to monitor my vitals and be able to report to my doctor in real time of my conditions.
The robot may also be able to prolong my life by working out the best level of exercise I should be doing and what foods and how much I should be eating to improve my health.
I also see that robots will become a companion for humanity and assist us where we need help but at the same time laws will change.
Robots will also assist us with reaching the stars and help us to expand beyond earth and even the solar system.
I believe robots will make my life easier and more efficient. And allow me to spend more time with my family and friends.
