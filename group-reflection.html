Group Reflection

This group functioned well in my opinion we had slow start but that would be expected with this being our first group assignment
and when we had to form the group most of us were bogged down with our Programming assignments but once we got started, we worked as
well-oiled machine we delegate the work between us all focusing on our strengths and the areas we feel most comfortable.
The part I found most surprising is how well we worked there was not a single agreement and when handed out the tasks for everyone
no one complained we got everything done. The aspect we could have done better is our time management with a slow start and the time
zone different between our team we had to do three weeks of work in one at a time that suited us all so we were limited in the times we could meet.
I’ve learned working with this group we all worked well together unlike groups I work with in the past we all did our assigned
parts and we all got along cant wait to keep working with them.
-Callum

time is the most important fact to us. Further, team effort that everyone put through the completion of assignment is the other most critical success factor.
Diversified skills set of team members further facilitate to good quality work.

Our team members from different states in Australia and from the US as well. Different time frame different continents are not barriers anymore.
As a team, by completing this assignment we all realized that the team work facilitate to bring different skills set to one place.

Moreover git-hub sophisticated technology platform provided us best support to complete this assignment.

Everyone in the group was easy to have a student team relationship with and we were able to quickly build a strong repour.
At first it was difficult to work out a schedule with the team and we lost two weeks of work time. We were able to evenly delegate
the workload throughout passing the torch to lessen the workload on each other or place too much work on one person.
For me, using Microsoft Teams as a learning tool was a great experience. It was a seamless classroom like experience and was as
though we were really in a classroom session. At first, it was difficult to work around each other's schedules,
however we were able to be pro-active and work out a time that suited all of us to be online.
The most surprising part of this for me was that the team was still able to work with me while I am overseas in New York.
Working on groups showed me that while I may have some ideas about a given subject, working in groups allowed me to see concepts
from a unique perspective and be able to develop ideas to be far more developed and coherent.
-Rhys Working as a team has helped me to develop my my skills working in an online business setting.
Since I am interested in working as a developer from home, being able to get exposure to work with new people
online has given me more confidence in meeting with clients when it come time to develop their projects.
Development is one part but another and very important part is being able to communicate and do business online.
I think we all got along well as a team and we’re open to discussion without conflict we all knew there was no time for that.

A surprising part of this project was having one student overseas and this meant that we all had to coordinate at
